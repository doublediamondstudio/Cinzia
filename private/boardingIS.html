How to board a flight in the airports around the world

KLM wanted to implement the digital boarding process to all the international airports where it flies.




Timeline
2020 - 2 weeks

Client
KLM / Air France

Tools
Bluejeans, Remote user council, Google analytics, Google Insights, Analytics 360


Team
Product Owner, Data analyst, Business Analyst

My role
User research, Data analysis, User council



Goal

With the new digital boarding process currently being developed for Amsterdam, KLM wanted to upscale it to the international airports.

My goal was to understand the specificities of international stations to tailor the new digital boarding process to them.
A secondary goal was to verify the satisfaction of the new boarding pass scan functionality.

The business goal was also to supply the agents with a secondary tool to board passengers, together with the pillars.

How might we tailor the whole digital boarding process to the international stations?



Research

Objectives

After the usability testing during the design sprint, there were still some uncertanties in the process and some missing information.

Thus, I organized the research, in order to find out:
  • The specific rulings and regulations of the entire boarding operations
  • The different phases of the boarding operations
  • The role of the agents in the critical decisions
  • The organizational and procedural differences in the International Stations
  • All the digital and non digital tools currently used

    • Data analysis

    Together with my colleagues from the insights team and the business analyst, I analyzed the data on the usage of the boarding functionality in Amsterdam and at the international airports, looking at adoption, engagement, user behavior and possible bottlenecks.


  • Remote user council with the beta users from international stations

    • Remote international user council

    Now that the boarding process at Schiphol was clear to me, I had to learn the specificities of the process at the international stations.
    • Time: 3 days, councils of 3 hours each, accomodating 3 different time zones: Asia Pacific; Europe, Middle-East and Africa; North, Central and South America
    • Participants: 12 station managers
    • Goals: understand the specific processes and regulations of the different international stations.
    • Goals: verify my assumptions, fill in the gaps, clarify my understanding of the process, grasp the goals of the interactions with the staff working in other areas.


      • Remote user council



      Findings

      The shadowing and interviewing of gate agents gave me several useful insights that I cannot share here in detail as they refer to internal KLM procedures.

      The research deepened my knowledge on the:
      • different phases of the boarding process
      • information needed at each different stage
      • specific tasks and actions to be done by the agents
      • rules and regulations

        • “Now we can go on the plane and board a passenger”

        — A senior gate agent

        The extensive research gave me a large quantity insights, that I organized in an affinity diagram in order to split the complex issues into smaller ones.

        I then created the user journey of the gate agents, taking into account the interactions with the other roles.

        This highlighted that the boarding process can be broken down into 3 phases : the boarding preparation, the boarding itself and the finalization of the flight in order to close the gate.

        As the results of my research refer to internal KLM procedures, I cannot share here any details.


        Beta release User interview

        We shipped the new functionality to our beta users and, after a pilot of 2 weeks, I organized some interviews to get their feedback
        • Time: 1 days, 5 interviews of 30 minutes each
        • Participants: 4 senior gate agents, 1 gate agent
        • Goals: gather feedback on the functionality and diagnose eventual problems
